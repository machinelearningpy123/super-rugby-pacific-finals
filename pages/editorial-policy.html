Editorial Policy

Super Rugby Pacific Finals aims to keep each page clear, useful, and easy to verify. This policy explains how updates, sourcing, corrections, and external links are handled on the site.

How updates are written

We try to separate confirmed developments from background context. If a page covers a match, league, app, tool, or product, the goal is to tell readers what changed and why it matters without padding the copy.

How sources are treated

  • Primary or official sources come first when they are available.
  • Secondary reporting is used for added context, not to replace the original source.
  • If information is uncertain, the page should say so instead of pretending the point is settled.

Corrections and updates

If you spot an error or a page needs updating, send the details to machinelearningpy123@gmail.com. Corrections should be reviewed and folded into the page as quickly as practical.

External links

Some pages link to outside articles, downloads, or related resources. Those links are added because they may help the reader continue, not because every external page is endorsed in full.

Independence

Unless a page clearly states otherwise, Super Rugby Pacific Finals is an independent site and is not the official home of third-party leagues, clubs, apps, brands, or developers.
