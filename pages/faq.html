Super Rugby Pacific Finals FAQ

These are the key questions people ask once the finals bracket starts moving and the format begins to matter as much as the scoreline.

How does the Super Rugby Pacific finals format work in 2026? +

The official format uses three qualifying finals between 1 v 6, 2 v 5, and 3 v 4. The three winners plus the highest-ranked losing team move into the semi-finals, and the top-ranked semi-final winner hosts the final.

Who is already through to the semi-finals? +

As of June 5, 2026, the Hurricanes are through after beating the ACT Brumbies 66-12 in Wellington.

What does the highest-ranked losing team rule mean? +

It means one team can still reach the semi-finals even after losing a qualifying final, but in 2026 that side drops to the fourth seed and cannot host a semi-final or the final.

Are the remaining qualifiers still to be played? +

Yes. The Crusaders host the Blues in Christchurch on Saturday, June 6, 2026, and the Chiefs host the Queensland Reds in Hamilton later the same day, according to the official finals schedule.
