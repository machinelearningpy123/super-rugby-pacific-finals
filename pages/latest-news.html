Latest Super Rugby Pacific Finals News

Read AnalysisFAQBonus for You

As of Friday, June 5, 2026, one part of the Super Rugby Pacific bracket is settled and two parts remain open. The Hurricanes are through to the semi-finals after a dominant 66-12 win over the ACT Brumbies in Wellington. The Crusaders v Blues and Chiefs v Queensland Reds qualifiers will decide the rest of the live week-one picture on Saturday, June 6, 2026.

This page uses official Super Rugby Pacific pages as the source of truth. When fresh results land, the bracket should be updated from the competition site first.

The main result so far

The official match report says the Hurricanes beat the Brumbies 66-12, becoming the first side to book a semi-final place. It was not a narrow escape or a late swing. It was a one-sided result that cleared one path in the bracket immediately.

Why the remaining matches still have extra tension

The official finals structure keeps three winners and one losing qualifier alive. That means Saturday is not just about winning; it is also about how teams lose, what seed they hold, and whether they stay in control of their route. The 2026 competition note is important here because the highest-ranked losing team is dropped to the fourth seed and cannot host after that.

What to watch next

  • Whether the Crusaders or Blues lock in a cleaner route by winning outright.
  • Whether the Chiefs justify their seed at home against Queensland.
  • Whether the surviving losing qualifier stays close enough to make the rule matter in practice.
